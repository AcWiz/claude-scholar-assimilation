# Machine Learning for Phase-Resolved Reconstruction of Nonlinear Ocean Wave Surface Elevations from Sparse Remote Sensing Data

## TL;DR
U-Net and Fourier neural operator architectures enable accurate wave surface reconstruction from sparse radar data with good generalization to different sea states.

## Research Question
How can neural networks reconstruct phase-resolved ocean wave surfaces from sparse radar measurements in real-time?

## Main Contributions
1. Proposes neural network approach for phase-resolved wave surface reconstruction from sparse X-band radar data
2. Compares U-Net and Fourier neural operator (FNO) architectures for radar inversion
3. FNO demonstrates superior performance due to global approach to learning mapping in Fourier space

## Method
Neural networks (U-Net and FNO) trained on synthetic realistic training data generated by high-order spectral method and geometric radar modeling. Training uses spatio-temporal radar data with multiple historic radar snapshots in each input. Models learn mapping from sparse radar intensity to wave surface elevation. FNO's global Fourier space approach better handles data structure imposed by wave physics.

## Datasets
- Synthetic wave data from high-order spectral method (HOSM)
- Simulated X-band radar backscatter data

## Core Results
- Both models deliver accurate wave reconstruction results
- Good generalization to different sea states demonstrated
- FNO outperforms U-Net due to physics-aware global approach

## Limitations
- Validated on synthetic training data; real-world radar data may differ
- Performance depends on radar quality and sea state diversity in training
- Limited to 1D spatial reconstruction in this study

## Research Gaps
- Extension to 2D spatial wave reconstruction
- Validation with real ocean radar measurements
- Integration with wave prediction systems
